Assignment

Design a Twitter landing page for Buffer, to help increase business account signup rates, on a team of three UX Designers.

Market Research

To gain insight into optimized landing page best practices, we each analyzed a competitor and a comparative landing page.

Five factors for comparison: navigation, social proof, scannability, call to action, product demonstration

Design insights: repeated calls to action, concise text and white space, distinct content sections, responsive design

User Research

We surveyed 19 target users, and conducted five in-depth user interviews to gain insights into what industry professionals look for when researching new tools.

Our survey focused on demographics, context, twitter habits and behaviors, attitudes toward social media, tool research habits, and current challenges.

Sample Questions:
Is social media your primary responsibility at work?
Which phrase best describes how you feel about social media marketing?
Why did you choose your current social media scheduling platform?
How do you learn about new marketing tools?
What matters when you’re looking for a new tool?
What are the biggest challenges managing your company's Twitter account(s)?

Affinity Mapping

We synthesized our survey and interview results with affinity mapping, grouping together similar responses and data to pinpoint trends and outliers and further understand our users’ needs.

User Personas + Scenarios

After synthesizing our data, we each created a user persona and scenario using the same format: demographics, attitudes, story, goals, frustrations, ideal experience.

User Pain Points + Problem Statement

We used our survey results to pinpoint four recurring user pain points when finding and working with a new social media marketing platform:
Value: will it be worth my time and money?
Ease of use: will it be easy to onboard and get started?
Collaboration: can I use it with my team?
Validation: how do I know this is a good tool?

These pain points, along with Buffer’s business goals, informed our problem statement: Buffer’s current Pinterest landing page layout is not easily scannable and does not prioritize the most important content for user decision making—-ease of use and value.

Design Solution + User Flow/Information Architecture

Our design solution to the above pain points and problem statement is:
Showcase the user-friendliness of Buffer’s platform.
Demonstrate key platform features, such as scheduling options, group collaboration and performance analytics.
Prove the value of Buffer’s platform, through current user testimonials and industry facts.

Based on this solution statement, we diagrammed the landing page user flow.

Paper Prototypes + Testing

We conducting five user testing interviews with paper prototypes of three different layouts, to hone in on a final design.

Mid-Fidelity Wireframe + Testing

Our paper prototyping results informed the layout of our mid-fidelity wireframe, which we turned into a prototype and used to conducted seven usability interviews.

High-Fidelity Wireframe

The results of our usability testing informed design changes for our high-fidelity mockup. Most notably:
Change CTA to “SIGN UP” for more clarity
Add case study for further social proof
Reverse pricing plans, so Teams and Agencies is first
Add a chat box for users to speak directly with a representative
